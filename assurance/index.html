Seeking Assurance for Transfer

At Ghana Education Service (GES), “seeking assurance” refers to the process where a teacher obtains an assurance letter from a district or region they want to be transferred to.

The letter confirms that there is a vacancy and the district is willing to accept the teacher before the transfer is processed.

Understand What an Assurance Letter Is

An assurance letter is an official document issued by a district or regional education office confirming that:

There is a vacancy for a teacher in that district/region.
The district is willing to accept the teacher if the transfer is approved.
The teacher can now apply for release from their current district.

It is usually the first and most important step in the GES transfer or reposting process.

Phase 1

Check Eligibility Before Applying

Before seeking assurance, teachers must meet the following criteria:

  • Serve at least 3 years at their current station (common practice in GES).
  • Have good conduct and performance records.
  • Have no disciplinary issues.
  • Have supporting reasons for transfer (marriage, health, family, etc.).

Phase 2

Identify the Preferred District or Region

Before starting the online process, the teacher must:

  • Decide the region/district they want to transfer to.
  • Confirm there may be vacancies in that district.
  • Contact the Metro/Municipal/District Education Office (MMDE) of the preferred district.

The assurance must come from the district where the teacher wants to move.

Phase 3

Prepare Required Documents

Before filling the online form, teachers must scan and prepare digital copies of required documents.

1 Application letter for assurance
2 First appointment/posting letter
3 Promotion letter (if any)
4 Academic certificates
5 Personal record form
6 Current payslip
7 Confidential report from district director
8 Marriage certificate (if joining spouse)
9 Previous transfer letter (if applicable)

Phase 4

Access GEDP Assurance Portal

Online Process Includes:
  • 1

    Open GEDP website or access the link provided by GES and navigate to Transfer Portal.

  • 2

    Fill in personal and professional details.

  • 3

    Upload the scanned documents.

Information required in the form include:
  • Full name
  • Staff ID number
  • Current district and school
  • Rank and subject specialization
  • Preferred district/region
  • Reason for transfer

Phase 5

Submission and Online Verification

After submitting the online form, GES verify:

  • Vacancy in the requested district
  • Teacher eligibility
  • Authenticity of documents.

If approved, the district will issue an assurance letter.

Phase 6

Download or Receive Assurance Letter on GEDP Portal

Once approved:

  • The teacher receives the assurance letter electronically.
  • The district compiles names of teachers granted assurance and submits the list to GES Headquarters electronically.

Phase 7

Issuance of Release and Reposting Letter

Following the assurance, the final release and reposting letters are processed to complete the transfer.
