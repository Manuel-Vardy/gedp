Ghana Education Service Promotion Portal

The exercise is usually conducted nationwide when vacancies exist and approval is granted.

TEACHING STAFF

The GES Promotion Exercise is an internal process for teaching staff to be elevated to higher ranks based on qualification, years of service, performance, and success in promotion examinations and interviews.

Eligibility Criteria

Meeting the standard for advancement.

Phase 01

Eligibility Requirements

1. Minimum Years of Service:

An officer must have served a required number of years in their current rank before qualifying for promotion.

  • Superintendent I → Superintendent II
  • Principal Superintendent → Assistant Director II
  • Assistant Director II → Assistant Director I
2. Educational Qualification:
  • Must meet the academic qualification required for the next rank.
  • Higher qualifications (e.g., Bachelor's or Master's degree) may be required for certain promotions.
3. Recommendation:

Must be recommended by: Headteacher, District Director of Education, or Immediate supervisor.

4. Registration:

Eligible officers must officially apply and register for the promotion examination when it is announced by GES on GEDP website.

Phase 02

PROCESS OF PROMOTION EXERCISE

01 Announcement of Promotion

GES publishes an official circular announcing:

  • Eligible ranks
  • Qualification criteria
  • Application procedures
  • Deadline
  • Examination date

The circular is distributed through district and regional education offices.

02 Application Submission

Eligible staff must:

  • Complete promotion forms on GEDP Promotion Portal.
  • Attach required documents such as:
    • ○ Appointment letter
    • ○ Academic certificates
    • ○ Last promotion letter
  • Submit through their head of institution to the District Education Office.

Professional Advancement

Advancement is based on qualification and merit.

03 Screening of Applications

District and Regional offices verify:

  • Years of service
  • Qualification
  • Rank status
  • Performance record

Qualified applicants are shortlisted for examination.

04 Promotion Examination

Candidates write a promotion examination which may cover:

  • Educational policies
  • School administration
  • Leadership and supervision
  • Professional Knowledge

Phase 03

Release of Results

  • Result Publication

    GES releases results after marking and vetting.

Successful candidates receive:
  • Promotion letters
  • New rank placement
  • Effective date of promotion

Important Notes:

  • Promotion is competitive and merit-based.
  • Meeting minimum years of service does not guarantee automatic promotion.
  • Examination malpractice or falsification of documents leads to disqualification.
  • Officers must stay updated with GES policies and reforms.

The GES Promotion Exercise ensures career progression within the Ghana Education Service.

It encourages professionalism, competence, and leadership development among staff while maintaining standards in the education sector.

Essential Guidelines

Key reminders for success.

NON-TEACHING STAFF

Non-teaching staff include administrative officers, accountants, secretaries, ICT officers, procurement officers, laboratory assistants, drivers, and other supporting personnel working in schools and education offices.

The GES Promotion Exercise is an internal career advancement process that allows qualified non-teaching staff to move to higher grades or ranks based on:

  • Years of service
  • Academic and professional qualifications
  • Performance record
  • Success in promotion examination and/or interview

Promotion is not automatic; it is competitive and based on merit and vacancy availability.

Administrative Merit

Steps toward career progression.

Phase 01

ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS FOR NON-TEACHING STAFF PROMOTION

1. Minimum Years of Service

Example:

  • Administrative Assistant → Senior Administrative Assistant
  • Senior Administrative Assistant → Principal Administrative Assistant
  • Principal Officer → Assistant Director

The required years are stated in the official GES promotion circular.

2. Required Academic and Professional Qualification

Examples:

  • HND or Bachelor's degree (for administrative roles)
  • Professional certification (for accountants or procurement officers)
  • ICT certification (for IT officers), where required

Higher academic qualifications may enhance chances of promotion.

3. Recommendation from Supervisor

The applicant must be recommended by:

  • Immediate supervisor
  • Head of Department
  • District/Regional Director
4. Official Application

The officer must formally apply when GES announces the promotion exercise.

Phase 02

PROCESS OF PROMOTION EXERCISE

01 Announcement of Promotion Exercise

GES issues an official circular specifying:

  • Eligible ranks
  • Required qualifications
  • Application procedures
  • Examination date
  • Deadline for submission

The circular is distributed to District and Regional Education Offices.

02 Submission of Application

Eligible staff must:

  • Complete promotion forms.
  • Attach supporting documents such as:
    • ○ First appointment letter
    • ○ Last promotion letter
    • ○ Academic certificates
    • ○ Professional certificates
    • ○ Performance appraisal reports

Applications are submitted through the appropriate administrative channel:

Immediate Supervisor → District Office → Regional Office → GES Headquarters.

Service Excellence

Advancement is based on administrative merit.

03 Screening

GES verifies:

  • Years of service
  • Qualification
  • Rank eligibility
  • Certificates

Qualified applicants will receive SMS from IEPA confirming his/her eligibility for the interview.

04 Interview

The panel assesses:

  • Leadership ability
  • Technical competence
  • Communication skills
  • Problem-solving ability
  • Job responsibilities

Candidates who pass the written exam are invited for an interview; original documents are verified.

05 Results

GES officially publishes results through GEDP Portal.

Successful candidates:

  • Receive promotion letters
  • Are placed on the new grade

Important Notes:

  • Promotion depends on availability of vacancies.
  • Meeting minimum service years does not guarantee promotion.
  • Falsification of documents leads to disqualification.
  • Promotion is based on merit and transparency.

The GES Promotion Exercise for Non-Teaching Staff ensures structured career progression within the Ghana Education Service. It encourages professional growth, competence, and accountability while strengthening administrative and operational efficiency in the education sector.
